# Mortgage — Curriculum

> **Status:** Draft. Teaching content for the mortgage module. Build out alongside the fundamentals knowledge check and the dashboard's Mortgage view, since both define the target outcomes.

## Target Outcomes (from fundamentals quiz)

A learner who completes the fundamentals tier can:

- Explain how a fixed-rate amortizing mortgage actually works — why early payments are mostly interest and late payments are mostly principal
- Read their own mortgage's numbers: original principal, interest rate, monthly P&I payment, current balance, and projected total interest
- Distinguish principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and escrow — and know which lines are negotiable
- Decide, with reasoning, whether an extra payment beats putting that same dollar somewhere else (high-interest debt, retirement match, brokerage)
- Recognize the wash-sale-style "gotchas" of refinancing: closing costs, reset clocks, and break-even months
- State their own mortgage payoff philosophy in one sentence — and know what would change their mind

## Structure (Draft)

1. **What a mortgage actually is** — a long-dated amortizing loan; the bank's view vs. the homeowner's view
2. **The amortization curve** — why the first 10 years feel like running in place, and why that flips
3. **PITI** — principal, interest, taxes, insurance; what's in escrow and what isn't
4. **Reading your own mortgage** — original principal, current balance, projected total interest, payoff date
5. **The opportunity cost question** — extra payments vs. retirement match vs. high-interest debt vs. brokerage
6. **Three levers: refinance, recast, prepay** — what each does, when each makes sense, what each costs
7. **PMI, escrow analysis, and the "wait, my payment changed?" moment**
8. **Selling, moving, and the hidden cost of resetting the clock**

## Tone

Plain, practical, never moralizing about debt. A 30-year mortgage at 3% and a 30-year mortgage at 8% are different financial instruments — the curriculum reflects that. Numbers are concrete; jargon is defined inline.

## What this module does NOT cover

- Home buying mechanics (offers, inspections, closing logistics) — separate module if/when added
- HELOCs and home equity loans beyond a brief mention — likely a sibling module
- Investment property mortgages and rental math — out of scope for fundamentals; a possible advanced topic

This module's job is to make the user fluent in the mortgage they already have (or are about to have), and confident in the prepayment / refinance / recast decision space.
